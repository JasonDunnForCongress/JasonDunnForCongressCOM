McMurry Software Engineer Announces Run for Congress in Pennsylvania's 14th District

Jason Dunn Enters Republican Primary, Refusing All Monetary Donations and PAC Money

McMurry, PA —

Jason Dunn, a software engineer and McMurry resident, today announced his candidacy for the United States House of Representatives in Pennsylvania's 14th Congressional District, entering the Republican primary ahead of the May 19, 2026 election.

"I am asking for your vote — not because I belong to the right team, but because I intend to do the work. Responsibly. Accountably. And lawfully. The families of southwestern Pennsylvania deserve a representative who is fiscally serious, morally grounded, and accountable to them — not to donors, not to party bosses, and not to Washington insiders." — Jason Dunn

In a move that sets his campaign apart, Dunn has pledged to accept no monetary donations and no support from political action committees, saying the decision allows him to focus entirely on the voters of the 14th District rather than the interests of large donors.

Dunn's platform centers on responsible governance and fiscal discipline, opposing deficit spending and what he describes as a "move fast and break things" approach to governing. On housing, he is calling for antitrust enforcement against private equity firms that have been buying up homes and pricing working families out of their own neighborhoods. On energy, he advocates for a diversified domestic portfolio — including expanded nuclear power — to meet the growing demands of the AI and data center economy while competing with China. He also supports professional, constitutionally grounded border enforcement and strict accountability measures for elected officials, including an end to insider trading protections for members of Congress.

Dunn describes his candidacy as rooted in Judeo-Christian values and says he is running to bring moral seriousness back to governance.

"We need a Congress that is as principled as it is populist, and that holds its leaders to the same standard on Monday that it preaches on Sunday." — Jason Dunn

Pennsylvania's 14th Congressional District covers a large swath of southwestern Pennsylvania, including Washington, Fayette, Greene, Indiana, Westmoreland, and Somerset counties. The seat is currently held by Representative Guy Reschenthaler.

The Republican primary is scheduled for May 19, 2026, with the general election on November 3, 2026.

For more information, visit jasondunnforcongress.com or follow the campaign on Facebook, X, Instagram, and YouTube.
